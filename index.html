About Me

Hello! I'm Hirsa Kia, a Computer Science PhD student at Temple University. Under the supervision of Krishna Kant, I work on reconfigurable intra-body networks. Previously, I completed my MSc in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Tehran, working at CAST lab on humanoid robots under Aghil Yousefi-Koma.

Research & Projects

Intra-body Networks (CDMNs) ▼
Energy Transfer Strategies (WoWMoM, 2025) ▼
  • A single on-body node must distribute limited wireless power via Magnetic Resonance Communication (MRC) to multiple implanted hubs and sensors over long horizons.
  • We build a calibrated energy-centric simulation of Chronic Disease Management Networks (CDMNs) and compare centralized, decentralized, and hybrid allocation strategies, revealing how each trades off efficiency, robustness, and control overhead.
  • Results appear in WoWMoM 2025.
  • Ongoing work formulates subset selection as a contextual bandit (Linear Thompson Sampling), which adapts to non-stationary channels and outperforms all three baselines while keeping computation tractable.
Energy–Accuracy Tradeoff (CHASE, 2025) ▼
  • CDMNs must minimize communication energy over MRC links while preserving physiological signal fidelity at the hub.
  • We design a closed-loop Kalman-filter controller where sensors transmit only when the hub's prediction error exceeds a dynamic threshold, achieving 30–67% fewer transmissions with 88–94% reconstruction accuracy (CHASE 2025).
  • I am generalizing this into a selective communication framework with a sensor-side classifier, a hub-side predictor, and a manager that updates thresholds.
  • The manager uses two strategies: an instantaneous-error scheme based on innovation-energy statistics and a cumulative-error scheme based on CUSUM-style drift detection.
Autotuning of MRC Communications (DCOSS, 2024) ▼
  • Efficient power transfer over MRC links requires maintaining resonance between transmitter and receiver circuits.
  • We design an adaptive controller that treats capacitance as the control variable and uses zeroth-order gradient estimation to solve a quasiconvex optimization problem, published in DCOSS 2024.
  • Ongoing work studies network-wide retuning, where detuning can cause cascading failures due to energy redistribution.
  • Switching the retuning trigger from absolute change to a ReLU-based criterion prevents benign redistribution from causing undesirable activations.
Through-the-body Comms (WiMob, 2024) ▼
  • A comparative study of Magnetic Resonance vs. Ultrasound-based communication for deep-tissue medical implants.
  • Evaluates both modalities across key metrics including path loss, tissue absorption, data rate, and implant form factor.
  • Identifies operating regimes where each modality is preferable, providing design guidance for next-generation intrabody networks.
  • Published in IEEE WiMob 2024.
RL, Control & Dynamics ▼
SINDy+RL for Nonlinear Control ▼
  • Combines Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics (SINDy) with model-based RL to control nonlinear, possibly nonstationary systems (pendulum swing-up, time-varying oscillator).
  • Compact SINDy surrogate models are learned from limited data and embedded in a receding-horizon planner that optimizes action sequences.
  • Online (adaptive) SINDy-RL matches a neural controller's tracking error (MSE ≈ 0.18 vs. 0.17) while keeping interpretable coefficients.
  • SINDy-RL outperforms model-free DQN by roughly two orders of magnitude (MSE ≈ 11.15), illustrating robustness to drifting dynamics and data efficiency.
Multi-Library SINDy (In Progress) ▼
  • Standard SINDy uses a single fixed library of candidate functions for all state variables, which can be unnecessarily restrictive.
  • Multi-Library SINDy allows different state variables to use different, tailored function libraries.
  • This reduces computational cost by avoiding large, monolithic libraries while improving flexibility in guiding the identification process.
  • The goal is to maintain SINDy's efficiency while giving practitioners more control over model structure and inductive biases.
Macroeconomic Dynamics Discovery (Comp. Econ, 2025) ▼
  • Builds an explainable nonlinear dynamical model from quarterly FRED data (1960–2022) for unemployment, inflation, GDP per capita, and interest rate.
  • Transfer Entropy selects informative couplings, then SINDyC learns a sparse system of differential equations with the interest rate as a policy input.
  • Phase-plane analysis identifies stability regions, providing a qualitative map of regimes where unemployment and inflation remain stable versus where they drift.
  • Work appears in Computational Economics.
TD Learning with State Aggregation ▼
  • Temporal Difference (TD) learning is central to many RL methods but can diverge under function approximation.
  • Studies how TD convergence depends on state representation in a simplified setting with linear function approximation and state aggregation.
  • Clarifies when particular aggregations preserve or break TD's convergence guarantees in practice.
  • The analysis and examples are written up as a blog post.
Applied ML & LLM Systems ▼
Augmenting Optimizers with NARS (Scheduler) ▼
  • Uses the Non-Axiomatic Reasoning System (NARS) as a learning-rate scheduler that reasons explicitly about training signals instead of relying on fixed heuristics.
  • Loss trajectories, gradient norms, and validation performance are encoded as NARS beliefs; syllogistic reasoning selects phase-specific update strategies at each step.
  • Achieves roughly 75% lower final loss on Rosenbrock and accuracy gains of about 3.2% on Fashion-MNIST and 1.1% on CIFAR-10 over fixed-rate and OneCycle schedulers.
  • The scheduler provides interpretable explanations for each adjustment, illustrating how symbolic reasoning can act as a meta-optimizer for deep learning.
Quantized LoRA (QLoRA) Fine-Tuning ▼
  • Fine-tuned Mistral-7B-v0.3 on the IMDB dataset for sentiment classification using QLoRA with the MLX framework, optimized for Apple Silicon.
  • Trained only 10.48M parameters (0.145% of the model) with 4-bit quantization, producing a ~2.1 GB adapter against a ~3.5 GB base model.
  • Achieved 90.0% test accuracy, outperforming the base Mistral model (85.5%) by 4.5% on zero-shot classification.
  • Stack: Python, MLX, HuggingFace transformers, peft, and YAML-based hyperparameter configuration.
Relational-Discriminative Fraud Detection (3 Datasets, GNN+ML) ▼
  • Credit Card (284K txns): XGBoost with scale_pos_weight beats SMOTE-RF (AUPRC 0.8779 vs 0.8293).
  • BAF (1M accounts): Temporal split with dummies and an age-FPR fairness audit (0.03–0.24).
  • Bitcoin Graph (200K nodes): GCN → SAGE → hybrid pipeline (AUPRC 0.545 → 0.665 → 0.7539); t-SNE reveals "Fraud Islands" with peeling-chain detection.
  • Hybrid stacking (GNN embeddings + raw features) resolves core ambiguities and scales for blockchain forensics. Full notebook
Conversational Local RAG System (Llama 3.2 + ChromaDB) ▼
  • Built a local-first RAG pipeline for private document querying using Ollama (Llama 3.2) and LangChain.
  • Multi-format ingestion (PDF/TXT) with RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter for semantic chunking, stored in ChromaDB with HuggingFace embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2).
  • Chat history buffer enables context-aware multi-turn conversations with strict grounding via metadata-based source citations.
  • Fully offline pipeline with no data leaving the local machine.
Advanced RAG with Hybrid Retrieval & Cross-Encoder Reranking ▼
  • Enhanced retrieval precision by combining semantic vectors (ChromaDB) with keyword-based BM25 retrieval in a hybrid search pipeline.
  • Integrated FlashRank (cross-encoder) to re-score and filter top chunks, significantly reducing noise in the context window.
  • Added a contextualization layer that reformulates follow-up questions into standalone queries for improved retrieval accuracy.
  • Together these stages yield consistently higher answer quality than single-mode retrieval alone.
Agentic RAG: LangGraph Orchestration & SQL Integration ▼
  • Used LangGraph to build a state-machine agent that routes queries between unstructured documents and structured PostgreSQL data.
  • A "Quality Check" retry loop assesses context sufficiency and triggers query expansion and re-retrieval (up to 2×) when data is missing.
  • A dynamic text-to-SQL path lets the LLM query student records and grades directly from a relational database when appropriate.
  • Supports automated routing decisions based on database schema metadata and generates 3 related sub-queries for expansion.
Bayesian-Optimized Adsorption Modeling (JTICE, 2023) ▼
  • Models tetracycline adsorption removal using multi-walled carbon nanotubes functionalized with aspartic acid and poly-pyrrole.
  • Trains a two-layer neural network with Huber loss, tuned via Bayesian Optimization over neuron counts, learning rate, activations, dropout, and NADAM parameters.
  • Bayesian Optimization uses RMSE as the cost function to efficiently navigate the high-dimensional hyperparameter space.
  • Results appear in Journal of the Taiwan Institute of Chemical Engineers (JTICE) 2023.
