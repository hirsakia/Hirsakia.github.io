About Me

Hello! I'm Hirsa Kia, a Computer Science PhD student at Temple University. Under the supervision of Krishna Kant, I work on reconfigurable intra-body networks. Previously, I completed my MSc in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Tehran, focused on humanoid sensor fusion and robotics.

Research & Projects

Intra-body Networks ▼
Energy Transfer Strategies (WoWMoM, 2025) ▼
  • Problem: Distributing wireless power via Magnetic Resonance (MRC) to implanted sensors.
  • Insight: Compared Centralized vs. Decentralized vs. Hybrid policies.
  • Result: Hybrid policies recover centralized efficiency without losing robustness to demand spikes.
Energy–Accuracy Tradeoff (CHASE, 2025) ▼
  • Problem: Preserving physiological signal fidelity while minimizing radio usage.
  • Method: Closed-loop Kalman-filter controller where sensors transmit only when prediction error exceeds a dynamic threshold.
  • Impact: Reduced communication energy by 30–67% with >88% accuracy.
Autotuning in MRC circuits (DCOSS, 2024) ▼
  • Problem: Maintaining resonance in non-stationary environments.
  • Method: Adaptive controller using a zeroth-order gradient oracle for capacitance tuning.
RL & Nonlinear Dynamics ▼
SINDy+RL for Control ▼
  • Goal: Combined Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics (SINDy) with RL for swing-up pendulum control.
  • Result: SINDy-RL outperformed DQN by two orders of magnitude in tracking error while remaining fully interpretable.
Macroeconomy Dynamics Discovery (Comp. Econ, 2025) ▼
  • Method: Used Transfer Entropy and SINDyC to build an explainable model of unemployment/inflation.
  • Insight: Identified stability regions based on interest rate policy via phase-plane analysis.
LLM & Applied Machine Learning ▼
Agentic RAG: LangGraph Orchestration ▼
  • Stack: LangGraph, PostgreSQL, Llama 3.2.
  • Feature: State-machine agent that routes queries between structured SQL data and unstructured PDFs with a self-correction loop.
Quantized LoRA (QLoRA) Fine-Tuning ▼
  • Task: Fine-tuned Mistral-7B on Apple Silicon using the MLX framework.
  • Efficiency: Trained only 0.145% of parameters to gain 4.5% zero-shot accuracy improvement.
GNN-based Fraud Detection ▼
  • Method: Hybrid stacking (GNN embeddings + XGBoost) on Bitcoin transaction graphs.
  • Insight: Successfully detected "Fraud Islands" and peeling chains.
