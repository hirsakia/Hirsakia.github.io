About Me

Hello! I'm Hirsa Kia, a Computer Science PhD student at Temple University. Under the supervision of Krishna Kant, I work on reconfigurable intra-body networks. Previously, I completed my MSc in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Tehran, working at CAST lab on humanoid robots under Aghil Yousefi-Koma.

Research & Projects

Intra-body Networks (CDMNs) ▼
Energy Transfer Strategies (WoWMoM, 2025) ▼
  • Problem: Distributing limited wireless power via Magnetic Resonance (MRC) to multiple implanted hubs.
  • Approach: Compared Centralized, Decentralized, and Hybrid allocation strategies using energy-centric simulations.
  • Bandit Extension: Formulated subset selection as a Contextual Bandit (Linear Thompson Sampling), reducing reliance on imperfect path-loss models.
Energy–Accuracy Tradeoff (CHASE, 2025) ▼
  • Problem: Balancing communication energy with physiological signal fidelity.
  • Mechanism: Closed-loop Kalman-filter controller where sensors transmit only when prediction error exceeds a dynamic threshold.
  • Result: Achieved 30–67% reduction in transmissions with 88–94% reconstruction accuracy.
Autotuning of MRC Communications (DCOSS, 2024) ▼
  • Problem: Maintaining resonance between transmitter/receiver circuits under environmental shifts.
  • Control: Adaptive controller using zeroth-order gradient estimation for real-time capacitance tuning.
  • Network Fix: Implemented a ReLU-based trigger to prevent cascading failures during redistribution.
Through-the-body Comms (WiMob, 2024) ▼
  • Focus: Comparative study of Magnetic Resonance vs. Ultrasound-based communications for deep-tissue medical implants.
RL, Control & Dynamics ▼
SINDy+RL for Nonlinear Control ▼
  • Method: Combined Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics (SINDy) with model-based RL to control nonstationary systems (Pendulum, Oscillators).
  • Robustness: SINDy-RL matched neural controllers in error while outperforming DQN by two orders of magnitude in tracking accuracy.
Multi-Library SINDy (In Progress) ▼
  • Innovation: Allowing tailored function libraries for different state variables to reduce computational cost and improve flexibility in governing equation discovery.
Macroeconomic Dynamics Discovery (Comp. Econ, 2025) ▼
  • Data: Quarterly FRED data (1960–2022) for unemployment, inflation, GDP, and interest rates.
  • Discovery: Used SINDyC and Transfer Entropy to map explainable regimes of stability and drift.
TD Learning with State Aggregation ▼
  • Research: Studied TD convergence under linear function approximation to clarify how specific aggregations preserve or break convergence guarantees.
Applied ML & LLM Systems ▼
Augmenting Optimizers with NARS (Scheduler) ▼
  • Concept: Used Non-Axiomatic Reasoning System (NARS) as a symbolic meta-optimizer for learning-rate scheduling.
  • Result: Gained ~3.2% accuracy on Fashion-MNIST over OneCycle/Fixed schedulers with full interpretability of rate adjustments.
Agentic RAG: LangGraph Orchestration ▼
  • System: Built a state-machine agent routing between PostgreSQL and PDF data.
  • Self-Correction: Implemented a "Quality Check" loop for query expansion and re-retrieval.
Quantized LoRA (QLoRA) Fine-Tuning ▼
  • Implementation: Fine-tuned Mistral-7B using MLX on Apple Silicon.
  • Metric: Outperformed base model by 4.5% while training only 0.145% of parameters.
Bayesian-Optimized Adsorption Modeling (JTICE, 2023) ▼
  • Method: Used Bayesian Optimization to tune a multi-layer Neural Network for tetracycline removal modeling via carbon nanotubes.
GNN Fraud Detection (3 Datasets) ▼
  • Hybrid Approach: Combined GraphSAGE embeddings with XGBoost to identify "Fraud Islands" in Bitcoin transaction graphs.
