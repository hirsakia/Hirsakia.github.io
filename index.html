About Me

Hello! I'm Hirsa Kia, Computer Science PhD student at Temple University. Under the supervision of Krishna Kant, I am working on reconfigurable intra-body networks. I completed my MSc in Mechanical Engineering from University of Tehran, working at CAST lab on humanoid robots under the supervision of Aghil Yousefi-Koma.

Research & Projects

TD with Linear Function Approximation in State Aggregated Setting ▼
  • Temporal Difference (TD) learning is central to many successful reinforcement learning methods but can diverge under function approximation.
  • I study how TD convergence depends on state representation in a simplified setting with linear function approximation and state aggregation.
  • I wrote up the analysis and examples in a technical report, available as a blog post.
Energy Transfer Strategies in Intrabody Networks (WoWMoM, 2025) ▼
  • Problem: A single on-body node must distribute limited wireless power via Magnetic Resonance Communication (MRC) to multiple implanted hubs and sensors.
  • Approach: Compared Centralized, Decentralized, and Hybrid allocation strategies.
  • Ongoing work: Extending this via Contextual Bandits (Linear Thompson Sampling) to reduce reliance on imperfect path-loss models.
SINDy+RL for Pendulum and Nonstationary Oscillator Control ▼
  • Combined Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics (SINDy) with model-based RL to control nonstationary systems.
  • Learned compact surrogate models for a receding-horizon planner.
  • Performance: SINDy-RL matches neural controllers while keeping interpretable stiffness and damping coefficients.
Multi-Library SINDy (In Progress) ▼
  • Observation: Standard SINDy often uses a single fixed library of candidate functions for all state variables.
  • Idea: Introduce Multi-Library SINDy, allowing different state variables to use tailored function libraries.
  • Benefit: Maintains efficiency while providing more control over model structure and inductive biases.
